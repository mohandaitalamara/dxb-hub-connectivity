DXB Hub Connectivity & Contact-Rate Optimisation

An independent data-to-decision case study of the Emirates weekly schedule
Case study · self-initiated Live week 2026-W28 · DXB T3
What this is

Can we lift hub connectivity and the contact rate at the same time — on Emirates' own schedule?

Starting from a real week of DXB operations, I rebuilt the network, reconstructed the missing rotations, and solved a single optimisation model that retimes and re-chains flights. The result improves both objectives that usually pull against each other.

Space-time network model — one graph per aircraft sub-type

Reading the graph: each node is a flight leg. An arc links a leg to a clone of the next flight departing at a different time frame — the same departure offered at its ±60-min retiming options. A complete path through the graph is one aircraft rotation.

Objective — one balanced score

α weights the connecting revenue; β weights the total operating cost — off-stand parking, towing, retiming and tail swaps. Sliding the α / β ratio moves the schedule between peaking for connections and smoothing for gates.
maximize α·Revenue −β·( Off-stand +Towing +Retiming +Tail swaps )
α · Revenue — connecting revenue captured through DXB  α · Σ Paxo·Revo·go
Off-stand — remote-parking penalty, arrival + departure  Poff·2Σ offstanda
Towing — double-tow operations that free a gate  Ptow·2Σ towa
Retiming — minutes moved from the published time  Σ |Sk|·xi,k
Tail swaps — deviations from the original rotation  Pswap·Σ za

Decision variables

xi,k ∈ {0,1}leg i takes retiming Sk (±60 min)
za ∈ {0,1}rotation arc a = (i→j) used (tail swap)
starti, endirotation head / tail (source / sink)
offstanda ∈ {0,1}DXB stay parked off-stand (remote)
towa ∈ {0,1}long stay double-towed
selc, uc,r, goconnection selection & O&D indicator

Constraints

  • Cover Σk xi,k = 1 — one retiming per leg
  • Flow Σ zout + end = 1, Σ zin + start = 1
  • Fleet Σ starti ≤ Tailsf per subfleet
  • Turn δj − δi ≥ req − gap − M(1 − za)
  • Gate Σ contact(z, offstand, tow) ≤ D per frame
  • Cnx Σc selc ≤ 1,  go ≤ Σ f(t)·uc,r

Connect-time preference f(cnx)

Piecewise-linear score in [0,1]: worthless under 60 min (missed connection), peaks at ~2 h, decays to 0 by 6 h. Non-concave → modelled with binary connect-time selection.

Top O&Ds by weekly Pax (DDS)

Paxo × Revo × f(cnx) weekly passengers · unit revenue · connect-time score → credited only for the best connection per O&D

O&Ds ranked by Pax×Rev; the top 500 enter the objective. Null unit revenue defaults to $200; duplicates aggregated (Pax summed, Pax-weighted mean revenue).

Next step ML upside

Today the connect-time score is a fixed piecewise curve and revenue is a flat unit value. With richer historical revenue & market-share data, a machine-learning prediction model would estimate willingness-to-pay per O&D and per connect time — turning this proxy into a demand-accurate revenue function and sharpening every retiming decision.
